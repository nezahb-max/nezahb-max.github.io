Computational Electromagnetics & Numerical Simulation

Compact accelerators, applied magnetism, antennas & radar — modeled from first principles.

I am Dr. Nezah Balal, an electrical engineer and physicist working on high-frequency electromagnetics and applied magnetics. This page collects research projects in compact particle accelerators, permanent-magnet undulators, antenna design, and millimeter-wave radar — modeled and analyzed in COMSOL Multiphysics and MATLAB (alongside CST and HFSS), with selected designs experimentally validated.

Research projects

Compact particle accelerators & IFEL →

Inverse free-electron-laser acceleration with tapered helical undulators, modeled in COMSOL — MeV-scale energy gain over centimeters.

Applied magnetism & undulators →

Permanent-magnet (NdFeB) helical undulators and magnet systems — magnetostatic field maps, harmonics, and tolerance studies.

Antenna design →

Horn, UWB and millimeter-wave antennas and waveguide mode converters — full-wave COMSOL modeling with experimental validation.

Millimeter-wave & micro-Doppler radar →

94 GHz radar systems for remote sensing and target classification, with MATLAB signal processing and machine learning.

Magnetic-mirror traps & particle dynamics →

Electron confinement between two magnetic lenses — Boris-pusher tracing in the exact coil field, mirror condition and loss cone, plus a live interactive 3D simulation.
