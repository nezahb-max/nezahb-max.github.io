Radar · Sensing · Signal processing

Millimeter-wave & micro-Doppler radar systems

At 94 GHz, the short wavelength provides high phase and Doppler sensitivity to small motions. I develop W-band radar systems and micro-Doppler processing that converts motion-induced modulations into features for target classification — combining classical estimation with machine learning.

Applications

The same core sensor and processing chain addresses a range of published and ongoing remote-sensing problems:

  • Target classification & low-RCS detection — micro-Doppler signatures and machine-learning methods support classification of vehicles and other targets; ongoing work examines drone detection.
  • Remote metrology — velocity-independent vehicle-height estimation from the ratio of two Doppler components (bridge-collision prevention).
  • Condition monitoring — multi-fault detection on wind turbines and rotating machinery from their micro-Doppler spectra.
  • Human sensing — remote sensing of human micro-motion, including speech-related vibration.

Method

Work spans the full chain: waveform and antenna choices, time–frequency (spectrogram) analysis, matched filtering and high-resolution estimation, and deep-learning classifiers — implemented and validated in MATLAB, with experimental data from real W-band hardware. Antenna front-ends are designed with the same full-wave tools used for the antenna work.

Radar cross-section (RCS) modelling

How strongly a target reflects — its radar cross-section (RCS) — strongly influences received power and detectability and can contribute aspect-dependent features for classification. I compute RCS from full-wave electromagnetic scattering and, for electrically large targets, physical-optics (PO) methods, in COMSOL and MATLAB.
